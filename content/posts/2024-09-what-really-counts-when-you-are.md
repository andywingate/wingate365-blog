<h2>Budget awareness and time management</h2>
<p>You should have a basic understanding of differences between project management methodologies (like Agile or Waterfall) and get up to speed on the tools the partner uses to keep track of tasks (like Jira, Azure DevOps etc), but even more important than these matters is a desire to be aware of the project budget and being able to effectively manage your time in the context of this budget.</p>
<h3>Project budget awareness</h3>
<p>Consulting often involves juggling multiple projects at once, each with its own set of deadlines and deliverables. The project manager is your friend, make sure you are speaking on a regular basis for a quick chat. Each week the project team (might just be you and the PM) should be aware of key deadlines, budget at the start of the week, expected effort in the week and therefore estimated budget at the end of the week.</p>
<p>Mileage will vary naturally, some larger firms may take care of all this for you, and you just work on assigned Jira tickets! But more commonly it will be a huge advantage if you take the initiative to be aware of the budget.</p>
<h3>Time Management</h3>
<p>Balancing your time between client workshop meetings, preparing project documentation and configuring the system is critical. Partners will plan resources work in varying ways, usually there is a central system to track these matters and provide reports on expected utilisation etc. Speak to the PMs or other consultants about the best ways to see your planned activities and the rules of the road at your org.</p>
<p>With your budget awareness, you can have a sensible conversion with the PM or lead consultant. If an activity has a lot more time assigned than you expected, then check if you really understand the quality/scope of the output? The same in reverse. Project budgets are estimates, but under delivering is just as bad as over delivering!</p>
<p>Finally, itâ€™s worth a mention about balancing your time between billable work and your continuous learning. Be sure to prioritise tasks to help manage your workload efficiently and avoid burnout and deliver consistent results.</p>
