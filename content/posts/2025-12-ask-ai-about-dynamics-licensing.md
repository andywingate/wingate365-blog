<h2>The Big Picture: AI Agents Take Center Stage</h2>

<p>The most transformative change in this update is the introduction of <strong>Dynamics 365 Agents</strong>—prebuilt AI-powered automation services that integrate directly into your Dynamics 365 applications. Think of these as your digital workforce, handling routine tasks while your team focuses on higher-value activities.</p>

<p>Microsoft has introduced a new framework called <strong>Copilot Credits</strong>, which serves as the common currency for executing these AI agents across the platform. If you're using Premium licenses (Sales Premium, Customer Service Premium, Finance Premium, or Supply Chain Management Premium), you'll get <strong>1,000 Copilot Credits per user per month</strong> included.</p>

<h2>Business Central: Two New Game-Changing Agents</h2>

<p>For Business Central customers, this update is particularly exciting. Microsoft has introduced <strong>two new prebuilt agents</strong> designed to automate critical business processes:</p>

<h3>1. Sales Order Agent</h3>

<p>The Sales Order Agent is like having a dedicated assistant monitoring your customer emails 24/7. Here's what it does:</p>

<ul>
    <li><strong>Analyzes customer requests</strong> received via email to capture sales orders automatically</li>
    <li><strong>Engages in multi-turn email conversations</strong> to clarify missing details or present options</li>
    <li><strong>Checks item availability</strong> in real-time</li>
    <li><strong>Follows up with sales quotes</strong> when appropriate</li>
</ul>

<p>This agent essentially eliminates the manual data entry bottleneck in your sales process, ensuring no customer request slips through the cracks.</p>

<h3>2. Payables Agent</h3>

<p>The Payables Agent tackles one of accounting's most tedious tasks—invoice processing. Its capabilities include:</p>

<ul>
    <li><strong>Capturing and validating invoices</strong> automatically</li>
    <li><strong>Matching invoices</strong> against purchase orders and receipts to reduce errors</li>
    <li><strong>Routing invoices</strong> to the right approvers based on configurable rules</li>
    <li><strong>Providing dashboards</strong> for outstanding payables tracking</li>
    <li><strong>Enabling better cash flow management</strong> and vendor relationship monitoring</li>
</ul>

<p>Think of it as your accounts payable department running on autopilot, with built-in accuracy checks and compliance tracking.</p>

<h3>What You Need to Know</h3>

<p>To use these Business Central agents, you'll need:</p>
<ul>
    <li>An active Dynamics 365 Business Central license</li>
    <li>Copilot Credits (which can be managed through the new framework)</li>
</ul>

<p>The good news? The core Business Central licensing structure remains unchanged—Essentials, Premium, Team Members, and Device licenses all continue as before. These agents are enhancements to the existing platform, not replacements.</p>

<h2>Agents Across the Platform</h2>

<p>Business Central isn't the only product getting AI superpowers. The December update introduces agents across multiple Dynamics 365 applications:</p>

<h3>Customer Service &amp; Contact Center</h3>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Case Management Agent</strong> - Streamlines case creation, updates, and follow-ups</li>
    <li><strong>Customer Intent Agent</strong> - Uses generative AI to discover customer intents automatically</li>
    <li><strong>Customer Knowledge Management Agent</strong> - Helps scale your knowledge base with minimal manual effort</li>
    <li><strong>Quality Evaluation Agent</strong> - Assesses cases and conversations to improve service quality</li>
</ul>

<h3>Sales</h3>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Sales Qualification Agent</strong> - Automates pipeline qualification by researching leads, sending personalized emails, and identifying buying intent</li>
</ul>

<h2>Understanding Copilot Credits</h2>

<p>The new <strong>Copilot Credits system</strong> is worth understanding if you're planning to leverage these agents:</p>

<h3>What are Copilot Credits?</h3>
<ul>
    <li>A consumption-based currency for AI agent operations</li>
    <li>The number of credits used depends on task complexity</li>
    <li>Credits are pooled at the tenant level</li>
</ul>

<h3>Who gets credits included?</h3>
<ul>
    <li>Sales Premium: 1,000 credits/user/month</li>
    <li>Customer Service Premium: 1,000 credits/user/month</li>
    <li>Finance Premium: 1,000 credits/user/month</li>
    <li>Supply Chain Management Premium: 1,000 credits/user/month</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Pro tip:</strong> Microsoft recommends allocating credits to specific environments to prevent agents in one workload (like Finance) from consuming credits meant for another (like Sales).</p>

<h3>Additional credits needed?</h3>
<p>You can purchase additional capacity through:</p>
<ul>
    <li>Copilot Studio pay-as-you-go meter</li>
    <li>Copilot Studio Copilot Credit pack subscription</li>
    <li>Copilot Credit Pre-Purchase Plan</li>
</ul>

<h2>What's Been Removed</h2>

<p>In streamlining the guide, Microsoft removed several sections that were in the July version:</p>

<ul>
    <li><strong>Dynamics 365 Guides</strong> (standalone product section)</li>
    <li><strong>Dynamics 365 Remote Assist</strong> (standalone product section)</li>
    <li><strong>Microsoft 365 Copilot for Sales</strong> section</li>
    <li><strong>Microsoft 365 Copilot for Service</strong> section</li>
    <li><strong>Appendix G: Copilot Studio Offers in Paid Public Preview</strong></li>
</ul>

<p>These products haven't disappeared—they're likely being repositioned or consolidated into other documentation. If you're using Guides or Remote Assist, don't worry; they're still supported.</p>

<h2>Other Notable Changes</h2>

<h3>Dataverse Capacity Updates</h3>
<p>The December guide notes "Dataverse and Operations capacity increases and consolidation," suggesting improved capacity entitlements, though specific details would require deeper analysis.</p>

<h3>Improved Administrator Guidance</h3>
<p>The guide now more clearly states that users with Power Platform Administrator or Dynamics 365 Administrator roles in Entra don't require a license to configure and administer applications—a clarification that could save organizations licensing costs.</p>

<h3>Terminology Refinements</h3>
<p>Microsoft has tightened up language around Microsoft 365 licensing prerequisites, changing "Microsoft 365 Business, Enterprise" to the more specific "Microsoft 365 Business Enterprise" for Business Central read-only access via Teams.</p>
