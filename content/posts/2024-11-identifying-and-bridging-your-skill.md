<p>Transitioning from an end user role to a consulting role in D365 Business Central is an exciting and challenging career move.&nbsp;</p><p>As a consultant you need to develop a well-rounded skill set that not only includes technical knowledge but also emphasises communication, project management, and strategic thinking.</p><p>This post will guide you through methods and tools to assess your current skills and identify areas for improvement, making your transition to consulting smoother and more impactful.</p><h3><strong>Understanding Required Skills for D365 BC Consultants</strong></h3><p>Before assessing your skills, it's essential to understand what skills are expected from a D365 BC consultant. Consulting roles typically require:</p><ul><li><strong>Technical Proficiency</strong>: In-depth knowledge of D365 Business Central functionalities, integrations, and configurations.</li><li><strong>Business Process Understanding</strong>: Ability to analyse and improve client workflows and processes.</li><li><strong>Consulting Soft Skills</strong>: Strong communication, problem-solving, and client management skills.</li><li><strong>Project Management</strong>: Familiarity with Agile or other project methodologies, especially important for managing expectations and timelines.</li><li><strong>Continuous Learning Mindset</strong>: Staying updated with the latest D365 and Microsoft stack features and updates.</li></ul><p>Map these skill areas to your current strengths and weaknesses. If you're still in an end user role, think about which consulting-specific skills you're likely to need more time developing.</p><h3><strong>Methods for Self-Assessment</strong></h3><ul><li><strong>Self-Reflection Survey</strong>: Use a survey to gauge where you feel confident and where you need more growth. This survey should cover technical, functional, and soft skills.</li></ul><div>Here is an example self assessment survey, I've put together to help you get started and gauge your overall know how from a mostly high level functional perspective:&nbsp;&nbsp;<a href="https://github.com/andywingate/files/blob/main/D365BC%20SelfSurvey.xlsx">files/D365BC SelfSurvey.xlsx at main · andywingate/files · GitHub</a>.</div><ul><li><strong>Experience Matrix</strong>: List all the D365 BC skills required by consultants and rate your comfort level in each area on a scale of 1-5. Identify patterns in your responses to understand specific areas needing improvement.</li><li><strong>Goal Setting with SMART Criteria</strong>: Break down your improvement areas into SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) goals to create a focused development plan.&nbsp;</li></ul><h3><strong>Tools and Resources for Skill Gap Analysis</strong></h3><p>Several tools and resources can help deepen your understanding of required skills and identify gaps:</p><ul><li><strong>Microsoft Learn</strong>: Offers self-paced courses in D365 Business Central, including certifications. Courses cover both technical aspects and business application fundamentals, making it an ideal starting point.&nbsp;<a href="https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/browse/?expanded=dynamics-365&amp;products=dynamics-business-central&amp;resource_type=learning%20path">Browse all courses, learning paths, and modules - Training | Microsoft Learn</a></li><li><strong>LinkedIn Learning</strong>: Provides industry-standard courses on essential consulting skills, such as project management and communication. Target courses that align with both your technical and soft skill gaps.&nbsp;<a href="https://www.linkedin.com/learning/paths/professional-soft-skills-learning-pathway">Professional Soft Skills Learning Pathway | LinkedIn Learning</a></li><li><strong>Community Engagement</strong>: Joining forums, attending webinars, and participating in user groups allow you to gain insight into consultant challenges and solutions. D365 community groups often host skill-based discussions and offer mentoring opportunities.</li></ul><h3><strong>Actionable Steps to Close Your Skill Gaps</strong></h3><ul><li><strong>Certification Pathways</strong>: Set a timeline to complete relevant D365 certifications, which can serve as structured paths for skill development and demonstrate proficiency to potential employers.&nbsp;<a href="https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/credentials/browse/?expanded=dynamics-365&amp;roles=functional-consultant">Browse Credentials | Microsoft Learn</a></li><li><strong>Hands-On Practice</strong>: Use a Business Central sandbox environment to explore configurations, workflows, and integrations. Practical experience reinforces your theoretical understanding.&nbsp;<a href="https://cdx.transform.microsoft.com/login?redirect_url=%2F">Microsoft Customer Digital Experiences</a>. Or just sign up for a trial:&nbsp;<a href="https://www.microsoft.com/en-gb/dynamics-365/products/business-central">Business Central | Microsoft Dynamics 365</a></li><li><strong>Join Peer Networks</strong>: Collaborate with other early-career consultants or experienced mentors to share best practices and gain advice on developing key consulting skills.</li></ul><h3><strong>Continuous Evaluation and Iteration</strong></h3><p>Skill development is ongoing. Schedule regular check-ins, perhaps quarterly, to evaluate your progress and recalibrate your learning plan. As you acquire new skills, reassess your comfort level and set new goals. Consulting roles require agility, so developing a habit of periodic skill assessments will serve you well.</p><p><br /></p>
