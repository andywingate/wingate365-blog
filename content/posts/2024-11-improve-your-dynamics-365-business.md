<div style="text-align: center;"></div><p>Microsoft have an ongoing mission around security, check out the&nbsp;<a href="https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/trust-center/security/secure-future-initiative">Secure Future Initiative | Microsoft</a>&nbsp;&amp; the recent newsletter&nbsp;<a href="https://cdn-dynmedia-1.microsoft.com/is/content/microsoftcorp/microsoft/final/en-us/microsoft-brand/documents/SFI_November_2024_update.pdf">SFI_November_2024_update.pdf</a></p><p>But here I wanted to share a very simple tip around an effective <b>just-in-time</b> access control for clients to allow / prevent partners from accessing their BC environments.</p><p>I see this coming into play post go-live. Better to leave the door locked and provide access only when required, so long as you (the client organisation) have suitable coverage with you internal IT resource/team to let the partners in when necessary.&nbsp;</p><h3 style="text-align: left;">Recent evolution of partner access in general</h3><div>Today we have GDAP access&nbsp;<a href="https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/partner-center/customers/gdap-introduction">Granular delegated admin privileges (GDAP) introduction - Partner Center | Microsoft Learn</a>&nbsp;which allows clients to grant partners access to their tenancy with only the necessary roles. For example on BC-related matters all you really need to be granting is&nbsp;<a href="https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/entra/identity/role-based-access-control/permissions-reference#dynamics-365-business-central-administrator" target="_blank">Dynamics 365 Business Central Administrator</a>. You might see partners also requesting <a href="https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/entra/identity/role-based-access-control/permissions-reference#service-support-administrator" target="_blank">Service Support Administrator</a>&nbsp;as this allows the partner to manage service health and submit tickets on your behalf.&nbsp;</div><div><br /></div><div>Before GDAP there was DAP, the main drawback of this was you had to grant Global Admin to the partner, which is pretty bad for security here is what ChatGPT has to say, I think mostly correct&nbsp;<a href="https://chatgpt.com/share/673cd880-d954-8002-b2ec-36845389ff7c">ChatGPT - DAP vs GDAP Comparison</a>&nbsp;in short:</div><div><div><ul style="text-align: left;"><li>DAP: Full Global Admin access.</li><li>GDAP: Specific, role-based access</li></ul></div></div><h3 style="text-align: left;">Partner access to D365 BC</h3><div>In the <a href="https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/dynamics365/release-plan/2024wave2/" target="_blank">most recent release wave</a>, client organisations have yet another options to help tighten security; with&nbsp;<a href="https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/dynamics365/release-plan/2024wave2/smb/dynamics365-business-central/control-partner-access-per-environment">Control partner access per environment | Microsoft Learn</a>.&nbsp;</div><div><br /></div><div>With this new feature (another from the the ideas forum&nbsp;<a href="https://experience.dynamics.com/ideas/idea/?ideaid=a96e938d-4b50-ed11-97b2-0003ff45c94c">Microsoft Idea</a>,&nbsp;<a href="https://experience.dynamics.com/ideas/idea/?ideaid=9757acbb-b7bd-ed11-9ac5-0003ff45e42e">Microsoft Idea</a>; thank you&nbsp;<a href="https://experience.dynamics.com/ideas/user-profile/?user=f720fb65-e248-e911-a95d-000d3a4f36ce">Kamil</a>&nbsp;&amp;&nbsp;<a href="https://experience.dynamics.com/ideas/user-profile/?user=bd48d319-b166-ef11-bfe2-6045bdfe9661">Xavier</a>) clients can toggle partner access off completely, or if they have multiple partners that they have granted D365 BC Admin to they can now define which partner can access which environment.
